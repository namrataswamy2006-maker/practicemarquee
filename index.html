Bandra Fort

Bandra Fort (Castella de Aguada) was built by the Portuguese in 1640 as a strategic watchtower guarding the northern sea route into Mumbai Harbour,
overlooking Mahim Bay, and armed with cannons.
It passed from Portuguese to Maratha hands in 1739, then later to the British, who eventually dismantled parts,
with ruins restored today into a popular historical spot for sunsets and view.


ISKCON

The ISKCON Temple in Mumbai, also known as Sri Radha Rasabihari ji Temple, is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna in Juhu,
offering spiritual solace, cultural events, and vegetarian food at its Govinda's restaurant, open daily with specific morning/evening darshan timings,
free entry, and a calm atmosphere near Juhu Beach.


Gateway Of India

The Gateway of India was built by the British in Mumbai to commemorate King George V and Queen Mary's 1911 visit symbolizing India's 'gateway'
during colonial rule, designed by George Wittet in Indo-Saracenic style, with its foundation laid in 1913 and completion in 1924,
later becoming a significant site for the final departure of British troops in 1948, marking India's independence.
